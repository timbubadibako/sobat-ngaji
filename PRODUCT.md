# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sobat Ngaji is for Qur'an learners who need independent practice outside direct guidance from a teacher, ustaz, or class mentor. Primary users include beginners, students, santri, university students, participants in ngaji classes, and general learners practicing on a mobile device in short daily sessions.

## Product Purpose

Sobat Ngaji helps users practice curated Qur'an phrases, listen to a reference, record their recitation, and receive early AI-assisted feedback. The product is an assistive learning tool, not a replacement for a teacher. Success means users can complete the core flow clearly: choose phrase, listen, record, process, review result, retry or continue.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a focused learning companion with a credible AI layer: respectful toward Qur'an text, approachable for beginners, and polished enough for an academic technology demo.

## Anti-references

Do not make Sobat Ngaji look like a generic green Islamic app, a banking-style navy-and-gold interface, a stiff admin dashboard, a childish gamified learning app, or a decorative landing page. Avoid visual systems that bury the Arabic phrase, overstate AI certainty, or make feedback feel like absolute judgement.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the practice task central: every screen should help users move through listen, record, review, and retry.
2. Treat Arabic text as the primary artifact, with generous spacing, strong contrast, and calm surrounding UI.
3. Make AI visible through feedback, waveform, progress, and processing states, not through noisy decoration.
4. Use evaluation language carefully: say kecocokan, evaluasi awal, perlu diulang, and rekomendasi instead of absolute right or wrong.
5. Build a reusable mobile design system with consistent states, component geometry, spacing, and semantic color.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. Support readable Arabic and Latin typography, clear tap targets, non-color-only feedback, reduced motion alternatives, and copy that remains understandable for beginners.
